About Butchery 101

A thank you note to the reader:

Dear Reader,


I purposefully spend as much of my time and resources as possible hyping up everyone else in the good meat system. Farmers, slaughtermen/women, butchers, chefs, cooks, dishwashers, anyone who is doing their part or more to improve our food system. They’re the ones who do the hard, underpaid and unpopular work. Often motivated entirely by altruism and kindness. That darn stubborn optimism won my heart years ago and I love it more every day.

But here I’ll tell you a little bit about myself and how grateful I am for your interest and patronage.

I care very much about the state of the climate, the lives and health of animals, an equitable and just environment for meat industry workers and farmers, financially, physically and mentally. My work in butchery and food comes from the desire to support people who I believe are doing the right thing in these arenas.

I learned my work ethic from my parents, two Filipino immigrants with a young family in a big, new country. I am willing to work hard and for long hours, but I strive to achieve some sense of work/life balance, comfort and peace of mind in this service/hospitality/food/meat industry.

When you patronize Butchery 101, you are directly contributing to the financial stability and future of a naturalized American woman of color (who, by the way, neither litters nor drives under the influence) via HER OWN small business. Companies in the U.S. which do that so directly are mid-rare; the meat related ones, rare.

I’m also really proud that many of the farmers I work with are women, often the majority stake-holder or lead in the business. I hadn’t set out to network with a bunch of passionate, smart hard-working women, but I’m overjoyed that that’s become the case. So when you support Butchery 101, you’re supporting them too. You’re welcome to check out our Partners page for more on them.

So thank you, kind reader, for your interest in and support of good butchery and business by women.

Butchery 101 is not an MLM and I’m learning how to do this small business stuff as I go along, so thank you also for your patience. For example sometimes it takes me a long time to reply to an email or message, but that’s because it’s literally just me doing the correspondence (and accounting. And booking. And web design. And marketing. All outside of my day job… Etc. etc.)

I have worked as a cook and/or butcher in excellent Seattle restaurants like Bateau, Matt’s in the Market, Crush, Cascina Spinasse and Volunteer Park Cafe. I now work in a restaurant as a butcher, but because the internet is such a weird vile place sometimes, I choose not to disclose my current dayjob.

Thank you again so much for your time, interest and patronage.

Best

Kristina Glinoga

Owner/Head Butcher of Butchery 101
